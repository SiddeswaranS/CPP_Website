Why power transformer configuration demands a constraint engine

Transformer design is a deeply coupled system: MVA rating constrains core cross-section; voltage ratio constrains winding turns; insulation class constrains oil type; oil type constrains cooling method (ONAN/ONAF/OFAF); cooling method constrains radiator count; tap changer type (OCTC/DETC) constrains winding arrangement; impedance specification constrains leakage reactance design.

When an incompatible combination reaches the workshop, the consequences are severe — a transformer wound to the wrong specification may not be reworkable. Scrap cost for a 100 MVA unit is ₹20–50 lakh. More critically, the delay violates contract milestones and attracts penalties.

CPP encodes IEC 60076 series and BIS IS 2026 constraints. The engine enforces technical compatibility before design is frozen. The derived specification includes winding data, core specification, oil volume, and BOM — all deterministic and traceable to the customer purchase order.

Three transformer sub-use-cases

Distribution Transformers (up to 2.5 MVA)

Standard ratings with BIS IS 1180 compliance. CRGO core selection, Al vs Cu winding, and tank dimension constraints automated.

Power Transformers (2.5–100 MVA)

HV/LV winding type, OLTC specification, cooling class, and impedance tolerance constraints enforced per IEC 60076-1 and IS 2026.

Special Purpose / Auto Transformers

Three-winding designs, earthing transformers, and rectifier transformers with application-specific harmonic and loading constraints.

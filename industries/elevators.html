The problem CPP solves for elevator OEMs

Elevator configuration involves hundreds of interdependent choices: shaft pit depth and headroom constrain car dimensions; car dimensions constrain load rating; load rating constrains drive type; drive type constrains machine room requirements; machine room requirements constrain speed; speed constrains safety gear type.

A sales engineer holding all of this in their head — while referencing EN81-20, EN81-50, and the NBC — is a recipe for error. A wrong drive selection creates a rework incident that costs ₹3–8 lakh and delays commissioning by 4–8 weeks.

CPP encodes every one of these interdependencies as constraint rules. The engine enforces them at the moment of selection — not after the order is placed.

Three elevator sub-use-cases

Residential & Commercial Lifts

Standard shaft dimensions with BIS-mandated safety constraints. Dealer-configurable without engineering review. BOM derives in seconds.

High-Speed / High-Rise

Speed ≥ 2.5 m/s triggers progressive safety gear, buffer type, and machine room constraints automatically. No manual cross-reference required.

Goods & Freight Elevators

Rated load up to 5,000 kg with special platform and door configurations. CPP enforces structural and drive interdependencies specific to freight applications.

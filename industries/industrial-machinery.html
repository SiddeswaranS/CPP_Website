Why industrial machinery configuration is uniquely hard

Industrial machines like CNC machining centres, pumps, and compressors are configured from hundreds of interdependent options: spindle speed constrains tool holder type; tool holder constrains coolant pressure; coolant pressure constrains pump model; motor rating constrains electrical supply; customer site supply constrains starter type.

Beyond product constraints, plant constraints layer on top: which parts are stocked at which plant, which substitutions are approved by the design authority, which routing applies for each plant's manufacturing sequence. All of this must be resolved before the MES order is raised.

CPP encodes both product constraints and plant-specific substitution rules in the same constraint graph. The BOM it derives includes plant routing, approved substitutions, and regional sourcing preferences — ready for MES without any engineering intervention.

Three machinery sub-use-cases

CNC Machining Centres

Spindle, tooling, coolant, and axis configuration with plant-specific approved-supplier rules. BOM includes tooling list and setup sheet.

Pumps & Compressors

Flow rate, head, material spec, seal type, and driver selection with hydraulic compatibility constraints enforced at selection.

Gearboxes & Drives

Ratio, torque, mounting orientation, and coupling constraints derived from application load profile. BOM includes lubrication specification.

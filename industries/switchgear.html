Why switchgear configuration breaks without constraints

Switchgear panel design is a network of interdependencies: incoming supply voltage constrains busbar cross-section; busbar rating constrains breaker frame size; breaking capacity constrains cable size; cable size constrains gland plate spec; enclosure class constrains IP rating and ventilation.

A single incompatible selection cascades through the entire panel design. When the error is caught at FAT — or worse, at site — the cost is ₹4–12 lakh in rework plus a delayed commissioning penalty and a damaged customer relationship.

CPP encodes these interdependencies as typed IEC 60947 constraint rules. The copper schedule and full panel BOM derive deterministically from a valid configuration — ready for purchase order and shop drawing in the same session.

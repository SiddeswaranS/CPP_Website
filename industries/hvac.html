Why HVAC configuration is broken without constraints

HVAC system design involves layered constraints: cooling tonnage constrains compressor model; compressor model constrains refrigerant compatibility; refrigerant constrains piping spec; piping spec constrains insulation thickness; electrical input constrains starter type; regional energy rating constrains inverter requirement.

Without a constraint engine, dealers call regional engineers for every non-standard combination. Engineers manually validate against product matrices. Quote cycles stretch to 2 weeks. Projects slip. Competitors win the order.

CPP encodes the full constraint network including BEE energy rating requirements and refrigerant regulations (HFO vs HFC by region). Dealers configure complete systems in 20 minutes with a valid BOM ready for purchase.

Three HVAC sub-use-cases

Chiller Systems (100+ TR)

Screw vs centrifugal selection, condenser type, cooling tower sizing, and COP constraints enforced automatically. BOM includes refrigerant charge quantity.

VRF / VRV Systems

Indoor/outdoor unit compatibility matrix, refrigerant pipe length limits, and simultaneous operation constraints modelled as rules.

Air Handling Units

Fan selection, coil sizing, filter class, and duct connection spec derived from airflow and static pressure requirements.

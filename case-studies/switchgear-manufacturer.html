The challenge

IEC 60947 compliance in 40 minutes — or ₹40 lakh in rework

The company manufactures LV and MV switchgear panels for industrial and utility customers: motor control centres (MCCs), power control centres (PCCs), distribution boards, and custom panel assemblies. Their product range spans a demanding configuration matrix — voltage class (LV ≤1kV, MV 11kV, 33kV, 66kV) × current rating (400A to 2500A) × breaking capacity (25kA, 50kA, 85kA) × enclosure class (IP41, IP54, IP65) × bus configuration (single, double, sectionalised).

IEC 60947 mandates specific breaking capacity minimums for given current ratings at each voltage class. IP rating requirements depend on site environment classification. Busbar sizing follows current density tables in the standard. None of these rules are optional — a non-compliant panel fails factory acceptance testing (FAT).

Their process: a junior sales engineer fills a quotation form, a senior engineer reviews for IEC compliance, a design engineer generates the copper schedule and BOM. Typical end-to-end timeline: 3.2 days. When the senior engineer was bypassed under deadline pressure, errors slipped through. In FY2024-25, six panels shipped with wrong busbar ratings. Average rework cost per incident: ₹8 lakh. Total annual impact: ₹48 lakh.

"A wrong busbar rating isn't caught until the panel is assembled and energised for FAT. By then, you're tearing down a completed panel. That's ₹8 lakh in parts and 4 weeks of delay — and the customer is watching."

— VP Operations, Switchgear OEM (name withheld)

The solution

IEC 60947 encoded as 12 constraint relationships

1

Constraint mapping — Week 1–2

Day 0 → Day 14

CPP's engineers mapped IEC 60947-2 (circuit breakers), IEC 60947-3 (switches), and IEC 60947-4 (contactors) compliance requirements into 12 constraint relationships. Each constraint is a typed rule that propagates immediately at selection — invalid combinations are never offered, not filtered after the fact.

Constraint Effect
VoltageClass = MV 11kV → BreakingCapacity ≥ 50kA Eliminates 25kA option instantly
CurrentRating ≥ 1250A → BusbarCrossSection ≥ 1600mm² Drives BOM copper schedule line item
Environment = Outdoor OR Coastal → IP ≥ IP54 Eliminates IP41 for outdoor/coastal sites
BusConfiguration = Double → RequiresBusCoupler = true Adds bus coupler BOM line automatically
2

Copper schedule derivation — Week 3

Day 14 → Day 21

CPP's BOM templates were extended to include the copper schedule as derived line items — busbar cross-section, length, and hardware calculated deterministically from current rating and bus configuration. This was the first time in the company's history that the copper schedule was generated automatically at quote stage — eliminating 4–6 hours of manual calculation per panel and removing the primary source of busbar rating errors.

3

ERP integration — Week 4–5

Day 21 → Day 35

The completed BOM is now pushed directly to their SAP B1 instance via CPP's REST API within seconds of configuration sign-off. Material requisitions are raised automatically on BOM push — the purchase team no longer re-keys line items from a PDF quote into SAP. The data entry step between quoting and procurement was eliminated entirely.

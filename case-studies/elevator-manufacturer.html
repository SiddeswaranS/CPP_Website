The Challenge

40 product variants, one overloaded engineer

The company manufactures passenger elevators across six load classes (6, 8, 10, 13, 20, and 26 person), goods lifts, and escalators for commercial and residential projects across India. Their installations span high-rise towers in Mumbai to IT campuses in Bengaluru — each project with its own shaft dimensions, regulatory environment, and fire-safety specification.

Their configuration process had one fatal dependency: a single senior engineer maintained a 52-tab Excel workbook containing every interdependency — shaft dimensions vs. car size vs. drive type vs. safety code (EN81-20, EN81-50, NBC 2016). This workbook was the company's de facto product knowledge system. When a sales engineer needed a quote, this workbook had to be opened, interrogated, and signed off by its owner. Average wait: 18 days.

When this engineer went on leave, quotes stopped. When junior engineers attempted quotes, errors happened. In Q3 2025 alone, three BOM errors reached the shop floor: two incorrect drive assemblies and one wrong door mechanism. Combined rework cost: ₹18.4 lakh. One customer escalated and the order had to be re-negotiated at a margin loss of ₹6.2 lakh.

"We had 40 variants, 200+ configuration rules, and one person who understood all of them. That's not a process — that's a single point of failure."

The Solution

Constraint graph over 8 dependency relationships

CPP deployed in 6 weeks from kickoff to first live quote. Three phases — constraint mapping, BOM template mapping, and user acceptance testing — each delivered a tangible handoff.

Phase 1
Week 1–2
Discovery & constraint mapping

Encoding 200+ rules into 8 core constraint relationships

CPP's solution engineers worked with the company's head of engineering to map every rule in the 52-tab workbook into 8 core constraint relationships within CPP's typed constraint graph. Each relationship was encoded as a formal constraint — hard constraints for safety codes, soft constraints for commercial defaults.

// Example — capacity constraint chain (EN81-20)
Capacity ≥ 13-person
→ Drive = Gearless VVVF
→ eliminates [Geared VVVF, Hydraulic]
→ requires MachineRoom = [MRL, Overhead]
→ triggers EN81-20.SafetyGear.Progressive

EN81-20 and NBC 2016 constraints encoded as hard constraints — they cannot be overridden by a sales engineer during quoting.

Phase 2
Week 3–4
BOM template mapping

Every valid configuration path mapped to a deterministic BOM template

Each valid configuration path through the constraint graph was mapped to a BOM template in CPP's derivation engine. CPP's deterministic derivation guarantee means the same input always produces byte-identical output — no engineer judgment required, no interpretation drift between sales and engineering.

The Results

90 days after go-live

Every metric moved in the right direction within the first quarter. By the 6-month mark, the finance team had validated the full-year savings projection.

Quote cycle: 18 days → 4 hours

The 18-day wait was entirely driven by one engineer's review queue. With the constraint engine preventing invalid configurations, no engineering review is required for standard variants. The quote is generated, priced, and dispatched within a single sales call.

BOM error rate: 12% → 0.3%

The residual 0.3% is not a configuration logic error — it traces to part master data quality issues (incorrect unit-of-measure entries in the ERP). The configuration engine itself has produced zero invalid BOMs since go-live.

Engineering bandwidth liberated

The senior engineer who maintained the 52-tab workbook now leads the company's new product development initiative — a 3.5 m/s high-speed variant for premium residential high-rises. The workbook is archived. It is no longer opened.

Order win rate: +22% in 6 months post-deployment

In competitive tenders with a 48-hour submission window, the company previously lost 3 out of every 10 opportunities due to quote turnaround time. With CPP, they now submit within hours. The 22% uplift in win rate is attributed directly to speed of response.
